♥ pottery is functional art ♥

Ceramics seamlessly blends art and function, creating pieces that are both visually stunning and practical. From intricately designed vases to handcrafted plates and mugs, ceramics elevates everyday objects into works of art. This union of form and utility allows artists to express creativity while producing items meant for daily use, showcasing the beauty of craftsmanship in functional designs.

things you need to know about pottery:

things about the kiln:

  • Kilns are specialized ovens that reach extremely high temperatures, allowing clay to harden into durable ceramic pieces. They come in various types, including electric, gas, and wood-fired, each offering different firing effects and temperature ranges.
  • Different clays and glazes require specific firing temperatures, typically called low fire, mid fire, or high fire. Understanding your kiln's capabilities and setting the proper temperature ensures your pottery pieces come out strong and perfectly finished.

things about glaze:

  • Glaze adds color and durability. Glaze is a liquid coating applied to pottery that, when fired, creates a glossy, matte, or textured finish while sealing the surface for functionality and aesthetics.
  • Chemistry affects the outcome. The final look of a glaze depends on its composition, application method, and firing temperature, making testing and experimentation essential for desired results.

things about the wheel:

  • Pottery wheels shape clay. A pottery wheel spins clay, allowing potters to create symmetrical forms like bowls, vases, and plates through a technique called throwing.
  • Electric and manual options exist. Pottery wheels come in electric models for consistent spinning and kick wheels powered by foot, catering to different preferences and styles.

sustainability in ceramics

  • Eco-friendly materials: Use natural, locally-sourced clays and non-toxic glazes to reduce environmental impact.
  • Energy-conscious firing: Optimize kiln usage with efficient firing schedules and consider alternative energy sources like solar or wood-fueled kilns.
  • Waste reduction: Recycle clay scraps and minimize water waste in the studio to promote sustainable practices.
